| The residential AVM currently lacks a way to distinguish different "price points" in the first model pass. In other words, at model initialization, a low-price home at $100K and a high-price home at $1.5M look the same, and are only distinguished by their different input features (location, size, etc.). By giving the model a starting "price point" to use, it can essentially break the market into different segments, each with different interactions. | [link](https://github.com/ccao-data/model-res-avm/issues/160) | Constructing a price strata feature or a lagged price feature doesn't work in this model. The `lag price` feature is equal to whichever of the following is first available: the maximum sale price in the last N years (excluding the current sale), the prior year's `BoR` certified market value, the prior year's Assessor certified market value, or the 2-year prior `BoR` certified market value. Two major problems were identified: sold and unsold properties are treated differently due to the 3-year assessment cycle, and the lag price feature effectively includes the outcome as a predictor in the assessment set. Scrapping this feature for now. | Modeling | 02 - 03 - 2024 |
